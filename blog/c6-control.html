When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

I find that for me, one of my triggers when programming is coming across an error or problem that I don’t understand and don’t know where I should look or what I should be researching in order to solve it. I think some of this comes from the fact that I’m so new to programming and don’t really have any kind of method yet for solving problems I get stuck on (since in other areas of my life that I have much more background and skill in, I don’t feel completely at a loss when I come across a problem, as I tend to have methods I use to solve such problems and paths towards learning more about what I need to know that I feel confident in relying on). When this happens I tend to feel frustrated very quickly and also helpless and defeated - a mix of anger and sadness.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

I like this exercise and I think to a degree I try to do something similar a lot of the time when I have strong emotional reactions. I’m not sure I’ll remember it by the acronym and ‘Siberian North Railroad’ though.
