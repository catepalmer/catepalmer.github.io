Motivation

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

Looking at life and the different kinds of happiness through this lens, it seems clear that a deeper sense of happiness isn’t really about pleasure or ‘chasing the next high’. It seems like it could be good to take a kind of top-down approach, starting with purpose and then working down from there, as Tan suggests - allowing passion (in the form of flow) to come from doing work that’s in alignment with your higher purpose, and allowing fleeting moments of pleasure to come to you through trying to follow purpose and passion.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, what are your core values? What do you stand for?

Living with integrity towards myself and others, being truthful, striving to learn and grow continuously.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

I found this exercise interesting - coming to the deliberate experiencing of the emotions associated with failure and success through the framework of the exercise and the book itself, I found myself less caught up in and overwhelmed by the emotions associated with failure, and I also felt less caught up in the emotions associated with success in a grandiose or self- congratulatory way. Both just seemed like things I’d needed to experience in order to learn and grow, and for that I felt grateful for both and able to hold them with some degree of calm.

Any other musings?

I had read about a lot of this research before, but it was still really useful to ground myself in it again and read it as summarised by Tan. I really enjoyed the exercise of writing about how you envision your life five years from now and found it genuinely really useful in helping me think about how I want to go forward into the future and what I want to focus my energy on in order to get my life on the track I envision for it.
